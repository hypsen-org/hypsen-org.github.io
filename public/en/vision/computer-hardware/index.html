Screen

To replace two aging 14" “flat” screens with poor contrast and black depth, I was looking for a single screen with the following specs:

  • VA panel1 [guide]
  • static contrast of 2500:1 or more [guide]
  • curved
  • about 34"

After reading some online comparisons, I restricted my selection to:

  • the Huawei Mateview GT 34"
    • brightness: 350 cd/m²
    • static contrast: 4000:1
    • resolution: 3440 x 1440
  • the Samsung Odyssey G5 32".
    • brightness: 300 cd/m²
    • static contrast: 2500:1
    • resolution: 2560 x 1440

The final decision, in favor of the Huawei, was based on contrast, resolution and the opportunity to get it while on sale.

Keyboard

Now working in a relatively dark environment, I made the choice to change the good old rustic Dell keyboard to which I was used for a backlit keyboard . The criteria considered were:

  • US QWERTY layout with numeric keypad, out of habit,
  • type membrane to limit noise,
  • lighting “through” the keys and not just “around” the keys,
  • sober look

Of the brands I’ve used over the years, Logitech and Steelseries had the best options. My choice finally fell on the Steelseries Apex 3 whose only fault is lighting “around the keys” which is too intense for my taste compared to lighting “through” the keys. I haven’t found keyboards that only have “through” lighting, no “around” lighting.
